Where was Treaty 1 signed

Treaty 1 was signed in 1871 at lower fort Garry in Manittoba. This place is near the Red River and it is not far from Winnipeg. It was a important place for trading at that time.

Who signed Treaty 1

Treaty 1 was signed by the goverment of Canada and the some of the First Nations groups.

Leaders from the First Nations and people from the government of Canda met to talk about the agreement before they singed it.

Why was Treaty 1 is Important Today

Treaty 1 is important today because it is an agreement that was meant to last forever between the First Nations and Canada.

Today many of the Frist Nations people still work with the goverment to make sure that the promises that were made in treatys are respected.
